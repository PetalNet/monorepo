# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who share precise, ongoing location with trusted friends and family, including across self-hosted Point servers. They use Point on mobile, often in motion or at a glance, to see whether someone is live, understand who can see them, and control sharing without surrendering location data to the service operator.

## Product Purpose

Point is a self-hostable, federated, end-to-end encrypted location-sharing network: “Matrix for location.” Success means trusted people appear and update live through normal mobile disconnects, the server cannot read location content, and privacy controls remain understandable and honest.

## Brand Personality

Bold, restrained, and trustworthy. Point should feel tactile and confident while letting the user’s people, location, and privacy state—not decoration or crypto terminology—hold attention.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble colorful consumer tracking apps, generic blue Material templates, decorative glass or shadow-heavy dashboards, or crypto-first interfaces that expose protocol mechanics on the happy path. Do not copy nexus visually; it is an engineering reference only. Avoid motion or color that could blur safety-critical presence and ghost states.

## Design Principles

- Tell the truth about liveness: durable state and visible freshness outrank best-effort animation.
- Keep privacy control one glance away and make sharing state unmistakable without alarmist color.
- Make encryption and federation work quietly; reveal technical detail only when it helps verification or recovery.
- Preserve identity across map, people, and detail surfaces with a small, consistent component vocabulary.
- Use restrained motion only to explain a meaningful state change.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support light, dark, and Pure-Black OLED appearances with high-contrast monochrome roles and no color-only meaning. Interactive targets are at least 48dp with explicit semantics. Presence is encoded by form as well as text. Honor both the operating system and in-app reduced-motion preference, and keep layouts usable with larger text.
