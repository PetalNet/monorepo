# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lab operators, maintainers, and administrators supervising a distributed fleet of managers, agents, hosts, work, signals, updates, and library artifacts. They use the console in an operations workflow where current state, provenance, scope, and safe recovery actions matter more than decorative presentation.

## Product Purpose

The Lab Console is the lab's all-in-one agentic management platform: the single full-stack place to provision, store, and manage agents and operate the fleet. Curated surfaces provide trusted lenses over one event and contract substrate, while the persistent assistant can compose new dashboards from the same scoped data. Querying and bridging other data sources is a secondary capability that extends the platform, not its definition. Success means an operator can provision and manage agents, find attention-worthy state, understand its evidence and freshness, and dispatch an authorized action without leaving the console or mistaking stale, synthetic, or cross-process data for truth.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, humane. The Good Place paper-ink metaphor should make dense operational work feel like a coherent neighborhood without hiding failures, uncertainty, or system boundaries.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS admin dashboards made from nested bordered cards, oversized metrics, and decorative gradients.
- Sci-fi command centers whose neon, glass, or motion obscures operational meaning.
- Cute or whimsical interfaces that soften failure states, replace standard controls with novelty, or use emoji as iconography.
- Mock-driven dashboards that imply freshness or authority the underlying contracts cannot prove.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence before reassurance. Show source, freshness, scope, and uncertainty wherever they affect trust or action.
2. The tool disappears into the task. Use familiar, consistent controls and reserve visual emphasis for current selection, attention, and authorized primary actions.
3. One substrate, many lenses. Every curated surface and assistant-composed view speaks the same contract language and preserves provenance.
4. Agents are present actors. Make ownership, availability, delegation, and the executor of an action legible across surfaces.
5. Recover honestly. Stale state may block assumptions, but it must not hide the recovery action when the responsible executor is live.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AAA text contrast across both light and dark themes. Do not encode state by color alone. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic controls, minimum interaction targets, reduced-motion alternatives, readable dense layouts, and phone/tablet fallbacks that keep the cockpit and recovery paths usable.
