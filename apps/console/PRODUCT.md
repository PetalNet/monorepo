# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PetalNet lab owners and operators working from a desktop or tablet while supervising agents, hosts, tasks, telemetry, security, and infrastructure. Their primary job is to understand what needs attention, prove the current state, and safely operate the fleet without switching between fragmented tools.

## Product Purpose

Lab Console is a dashboard-assistant and a set of curated operational lenses over one contract-backed substrate. It makes urgent work visible, keeps routine signal traffic quiet, exposes provenance for every claim, and lets humans and agents use the same named operations. Success means the operator can see what is true, what is stale, what needs them, and what action will actually run.

## Brand Personality

Honest, calm, and quietly humane. Good Place lore supplies warmth and a second read, while facts, familiar controls, and operational clarity always remain primary.

## Anti-references

Not a generic SaaS card grid, a neon terminal fantasy, a glassmorphic control room, or a novelty fan interface. It must not hide uncertainty, fabricate freshness, substitute lore for plain labels, or use decorative color and motion in the working path.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before reassurance: positive health claims require fresh proof, and uncertainty is shown plainly.
- Function first, flavor wraps: every label works without knowledge of the lore.
- One substrate, many lenses: surfaces share data, components, derivations, provenance, and named operations.
- Agents are peers: agent presence and agent-operable actions are visible throughout the system.
- Calm until action is required: density serves scanning, while interruption is reserved for genuine urgency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA text contrast on every permitted surface, keyboard-complete interaction, visible focus, 32px minimum targets and 40px primary targets, reduced-motion alternatives for every transition, polite live regions, no color-only status, and honest phone lenses for dense surfaces.
